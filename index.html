Why this matters

Many communities still lack reliable knowledge about clean water and sanitation. Preventable diseases like cholera, typhoid, and diarrhea persist due to poor hygiene practices. This site aims to bridge the information gap and make health knowledge more accessible.

How to Access Clean Water

  • Use safe water sources: protected wells, boreholes, or piped supplies.
  • Boil water to kill germs before drinking.
  • Use water filters or purification tablets when needed.
  • Harvest rainwater using clean collection systems and covered tanks.
  • Use community treatment options like chlorination where available.

How to Store Clean Water

  • Use clean, food-grade containers and wash them before filling.
  • Cover containers tightly to prevent contamination.
  • Store containers in a cool, shaded place away from direct sunlight.
  • Use a clean scoop or tap to draw water ‚Äî avoid dipping hands or cups.
  • Empty and clean storage containers regularly.

Hand Washing Techniques

  • Wet your hands with clean, running water (warm or cold).
  • Apply enough soap to cover all hand surfaces.
  • Rub your hands palm to palm to create a lather.
  • Rub the back of each hand with the palm of the other hand.
  • Clean between your fingers by interlacing them.
  • Rub the backs of your fingers against the opposite palm.
  • Clean your thumbs by rotational rubbing in the opposite palm.
  • Rub your fingertips on your palms to clean under nails.
  • Rinse hands thoroughly with clean running water.
  • Dry hands with a clean towel or air dry them.
  • Use a towel to turn off the tap if possible.

Ways to Prevent Waterborne Diseases

Follow these simple hygiene and sanitation measures to stay safe from waterborne diseases:

  • Drink only treated or boiled water from safe sources.
  • Use water filters or purification tablets if boiling is not possible.
  • Keep drinking water in clean, covered containers.
  • Wash hands with soap and water regularly.
  • Ensure proper disposal of sewage and waste.
  • Cook food thoroughly and keep it covered.
  • Clean water storage containers frequently.
  • Avoid using contaminated water for washing or bathing.
  • Report and fix leaks or contamination in community water supplies.
  • Spread awareness on hygiene and water safety in your community.
